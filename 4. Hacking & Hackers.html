What is Hacking & Hacker Types.

Hacking: Hacking is the process of identifying vulnerabilities/loopholes/flaws/weakness and exploit (take advantage of) those to gain an unauthorized access to the system or network.

Hacker Types: Hackers can be categorized based on their motives and intentions. Here are some common types:

  1. White Hat Hackers: These are ethical hackers who use their skills to identify and fix vulnerabilities in systems. They work with organizations to improve security and prevent malicious attacks.

  2. Black Hat Hackers: These are malicious hackers who exploit vulnerabilities for personal gain or to cause harm. They may engage in activities like stealing data, disrupting services, or launching attacks.

  3. Gray Hat Hackers: These hackers operate in a gray area between white hat and black hat. They may exploit vulnerabilities but often do so with good intentions, such as exposing security flaws to force organizations to address them.

  4. Script Kiddies: These are individuals who use pre-written tools and scripts to carry out attacks, often without understanding the underlying techniques. They are typically less skilled than other hackers.

  5. Hacktivists: These are hackers who use their skills for political or social causes. They may launch attacks to protest government policies or corporate practices.

  6. State-Sponsored Hackers: These are hackers employed by governments to conduct cyberattacks against other countries or organizations. They often have advanced skills and resources.
