Cryptology

  • Cryptology is the study of codes and ciphers. It involves the creation of secret messages (encryption) and the breaking of these messages (decryption). In simpler terms, it’s the science of secret writing.

  • Cryptology combines two main areas such as **Cryptography ** and **Cryptanalysis **

  • So, cryptology is all about ensuring that messages and data stay private and secure from unauthorized access.

What is Cryptography?

  • “Cryptography” is the whole of the techniques used to transform the transmitted information into a form that cannot be understood by unauthorized people.

  • Cryptography includes mathematical methods that try to provide the basic objectives of information security such as confidentiality, integrity, authentication, and non-repudiation.

  • “Cryptography” is the science that converts plaintext to ciphertext with the crypto algorithm.

  • Cryptography is the broader field that encompasses all the techniques and methods used to secure communication and data. This includes encryption, but also other concepts like digital signatures, hashing, and steganography.

Note: Cryptography is the toolbox, and encryption is one of the tools within it. So, cryptography and encryption both are not the same.

What is Cryptanalysis?

  • Cryptanalysis is the process of breaking a cryptographic system or algorithm. It’s an attempt to decipher a message without having the correct key.

Note : Decryption is the intended way to open a locked box using the correct key. Cryptanalysis is trying to open the box without the key, perhaps by finding a hidden lock or picking the lock. So, cryptanalysis and decryption both are not the same.
