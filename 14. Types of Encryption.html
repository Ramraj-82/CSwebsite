Types of Encryption

  • Encryption means to keep data privacy and confidentiality while transmitting the over the network.

There are two main types of encryption:

  1. Symmetric Encryption
  2. Asymmetric Encryption

1. Symmetric Encryption (Private key encryption):

  • Definition: Uses the same private key for both encryption and decryption of data.
  • Speed: Generally faster and requires less computational power compared to asymmetric encryption.
  • Security: Requires secure transmission of the key (Less secure).
  • Common Algorithms:
    • AES (Advanced Encryption Standard): Widely used due to its strength and efficiency.
    • DES (Data Encryption Standard): Older and less secure due to vulnerability to brute-force attacks; largely replaced by AES.
    • 3DES (Triple DES): Applies DES encryption three times for added security, but slower than AES.

Types of symmetric encryption:

  1. Classic symmetric encryption
    a) Transposition
    b) Substitution

2.Modern symmetric encryption
c) Stream
d) Block

a)Transposition: It is a method of encryption by which the position held by units of plain text are shifted according to a regular system.

b)Substitution: In this, the plain text replaced with cipher text according to a fixed system and the units may be single letters, double letters, pairs of letter.

c)Stream: It encrypts the plain text bit by bit by using 128 bit key.

d)Block: First, divide the data into blocks, each block will convert in to binary and then apply 128 bit encryption then convert into cipher text.
The receiver decrypt the cipher text by using same method.

NOTE: Block cipher is easier and faster than stream cipher.

2. Asymmetric Encryption (Public key encryption):

  • Definition: Uses a pair of keys, one public and one private. Data encrypted with the public key can only be decrypted with the corresponding private key, and vice versa.
  • Security: More secure as the private key remains secret.
  • Speed: Slower than symmetric encryption.
  • Common Algorithms:
    • RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman): Commonly used for secure data transmission and digital signatures.
    • ECC (Elliptic Curve Cryptography): Provides similar security to RSA but with shorter key lengths, making it more efficient.
    • ElGamal: Often used in combination with other cryptographic techniques for encryption and digital signatures.

Hybrid Encryption:

  • Combines symmetric and asymmetric encryption for optimal security and performance. It uses asymmetric encryption to securely exchange a symmetric key, which is then used for efficient encryption of the actual data.

Key Points to Consider:

  • Security Level: Choose an algorithm with a strong security level, especially for sensitive data.
  • Speed: Consider the performance requirements of your application.
  • Key Management: Implement secure key management practices to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Legal Compliance: Ensure that your encryption methods comply with relevant regulations and laws.

Note: Hybrid encryption is a versatile technique that can be applied to a wide range of services and applications where data security is a priority.
